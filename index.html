A commodity exchange is an association, company, or body corporate that organises futures trading in commodities and is registered with SEBI (formerly the Forward Market Commission).

There are 24 commodity exchanges in India. The two national-level exchanges are: MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange of India, Mumbai) and NCDEX (National Commodity and Derivative Exchange, Mumbai).

Commodity markets are open to individual retail investors, traders, hedgers, and institutional investors. Anyone with an account at a registered broker like BnB Financial can participate.

BnB Financial is registered with SEBI as a Trading cum Clearing Member of MCX. SEBI Code: INZ000057434 · MCX Member ID: 40735. Grievances can be filed through SCORES at scores.gov.in.

A futures contract is a standardized agreement to buy or sell a commodity at a predetermined price at a specified date in the future. These contracts are traded on commodity exchanges like MCX and are used for both hedging and speculation.

Hedging is a risk management strategy where commodity traders or producers lock in a future price to protect themselves against adverse price movements. For example, a gold jeweller may sell gold futures to hedge against a potential fall in gold prices.

Margin is the initial deposit required to enter into a futures contract. It is a percentage of the total contract value and acts as a performance bond to ensure that both parties fulfil their obligations. MCX sets the margin requirements for each commodity.
